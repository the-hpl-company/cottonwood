## What This Is

**The surface.** Sabia — the Brazilian sovereign model on the Maritaca substrate — is not being proposed here as a content engine. It is being proposed as a surface: a layer between a person's genuine taste and the infrastructure required to express that taste at scale. The distinction matters. A content engine produces. A surface routes. The product is the routing.

**The person.** Leonor is not a test case. She is the customer. The finding begins with her because genuine taste is not a demographic — it is a specific person with specific preferences, already paying for specific tools (Snow.ai), already doing the work. The MVP is not a new product pitched to a hypothetical user. It is a replication of one thing she already pays for, delivered through infrastructure she does not need to understand.

**The posture.** Customer discovery, not colonization. This phrase carries the weight of the finding. The failure mode in influencer-adjacent product development is the assumption that the builder's aesthetic judgment should replace the user's. It should not. The routing layer does not impose a path. It finds the path that already exists in the person's genuine interest and makes it traversable.

**The infrastructure.** Snow.ai is a filter product — AI-enhanced photo and video editing, primarily used by creators for aesthetic consistency. fal.ai is a generative AI inference platform with API access to image and video generation models. The MVP claim is that fal.ai can replicate at least one Snow.ai filter at lower cost and higher configurability, without requiring the user to interact with the infrastructure directly. This is not a product launch. It is a hypothesis to be tested in the next DRS cycle.

**The arm.** The HPL influencer arm is not a future initiative. It exists. The finding is that the routing layer — the thing that connects genuine taste to scalable production — is what has been missing, and that Sabia as a surface is a plausible candidate for that layer.

---

## What This Is Not

This is not a completed product specification. It is not a market analysis. It is not a commitment to build.

It is a signal, recorded at the moment of recognition, with sufficient context to reconstruct the reasoning when the formal DRS cycle begins.

The formal investigation will need to address:

- Whether fal.ai's current model catalog can replicate Snow.ai's specific filter outputs at comparable quality
- What Leonor's actual workflow looks like — what she produces, at what frequency, for what audience
- Whether the routing metaphor holds under pressure: what happens when genuine taste is ambiguous, or when the path it implies is technically infeasible
- How the HPL influencer arm is currently structured, and where the routing layer would attach to it
- The competitive landscape: Snow.ai, Lightricks, Adobe Firefly, and the consumer-facing generative tools that are converging on this space
